The gravitational pull of the Moon plays a crucial role in causing tides on Earth’s oceans. This phenomenon happens due to the Moon’s gravitational force exerting a pull on Earth’s water;leading to the periodic rising and falling of ocean levels, which we observe as tides.

The sun’s gravitational pull, while significant, is weaker on the moon compared to Earth's gravity. This reduced influence means that the sun affects the moon's orbit less directly, but it plays a crucial role in shaping tidal patterns on Earth. During spring tides, the sun and moon align, enhancing tidal ranges, while during neap tides, their perpendicular alignment results in smaller tidal fluctuations.
